Coco (2021)

A Video Calling Companion for Our Loved Ones.

As socialising online became the norm due to the Covid-19 pandemic, how might design bridge the physical to digital?

Coco is a video calling companion for our friends and family. It translates the non-verbal cues subtly present in our face-to-face communication to reflect the human presence lost from isolation.

During the pandemic, many of us would often video call our friends and family just to be in one anotherâ€™s presence and stay in touch while tending to our personal chores.

Designed with the intention for passive video calling, Coco aims to reflect the relationship we have with our loved ones at home, while far apart.

Design process and challenge: Influenced by the proxemics theory coined by Edward Hall, I explored with a series of prototypes ranging from physical products to digital filters that react to different levels of interpersonal distance and non-verbal cues commonly missed while video calling.
