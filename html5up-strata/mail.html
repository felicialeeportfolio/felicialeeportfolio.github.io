Kindle (2020)

To reimagine the experience of interacting with the mail package.

When the word "mail" is mentioned, what often comes to mind would be an archetype of a white envelope opening up to the content on the inside.

But what if the experience of receiving and “opening” the mail could be so delightful that it renders the content superfluous?

Through multiple explorations, our group discovered the potential of using wax as a material to reveal one’s message. Wax has the potential to morph endlessly between states, providing the opportunity of an infinite unraveling experience.

Exploration on spring mechanisms

Exploration on opacity levels of paper

Reflecting the romanticism of reading by the candlelight, Kindle is ideal for sharing a joyous message with a poetic touch. The illuminating flame sets the scene for a slow reveal enclosed in an intimate setting.

Kindle was part of the Forager Things exhibition at the National Design Centre (Singapore) from December 2020 to February 2021. It was also part of a guided workshop session to teach the public on how to make the mail.
