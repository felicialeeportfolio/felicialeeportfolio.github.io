Move (2019)

Done in collaboration with Govtech.
How might we design for new service concepts enabled by open data?

Open data can be extracted from all around us, from the duration of the sunshine to the location of different infrastructure. As a start, our group used open data as raw design materials and gradually shaped them into a relatable experience common to both you and me – transportation.

Transportation is an integral part of our life, and we may often find ourselves presented with a wide array of travel information on various platforms, while grappling to decide between the different travel routes, convenience, or price.

While taking all these factors into consideration, we designed a mobile application, Move, that integrates data to improve the decision-making process of price sensitive travelers.

Co-design workshop with participants

Usability testing using paper prototypes

It has 4 main functions:

1. Displays timing of multiple public buses depending on users’ preference
2. Compares prices across private hire companies and locates nearby pick-up points offering cheaper rides
3. Uses weather and traffic data to identify price fluctuations
4. Tracks users’ travel expenses to give insight into their travel habits



Design process and challenge: We went through a series of iterative process consisting of multiple interviews and co-design workshops using paper prototypes to identify the most user friendly interface and visualisations.

Skills required: Figma, After Effects, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, Premiere Pro, Tableau Software
Timeline: 11 weeks, Designed with Ng Ailing, Lee Zhi Ying, Chan Botter
