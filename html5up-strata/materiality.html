Steady Lah! (2019)

Done in collaboration with Govtech.
Can we design for material experiences of digital services?

A lack of understanding between providers and users would often lead to frustration and eventually discontinuation of the service, even though both parties could potentially benefit each other with a simple workaround.

In this project, our group sought to have a deeper understanding of the various interactions between service providers and usersâ€™ needs to design a new servicescape of digital services.

Through multiple surveys, interviews and co-design workshop, we identified a lack of proper transitioning for older working adults into retirement life. The moment one stops working, they find themselves having too much time, leading to a rise in health issues.

Targetting older working adults aged 55 and above, we designed an eco-system to create an easy transition from working to retirement life.

Different features can be found on the pamphlet

Potential users can scan the QR code to download the app.

Steady Lah! takes on a holistic approach in ensuring a smoother transition to retirement and comes in 3 main aspects:

1. Goals Setter - to help soon-to-be retirees connect with like-minded people & kick start their goal driven life
2. Health Calendar - to provide tailored healthcare information for Pro-active Health Management
3. Experience Exchange - to engage in community-based lifelong learning through the exchange of skills and experience



Design process and challenge: As part of our onboarding process, potential users will receive a pamphlet via snail mail. As they trace through the multiple paths offered in the pamphlet, they will be able to find a feature that best fits their current stage of life.

Skills: Figma, After Effects, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, Premiere Pro
Timeline: 13 weeks, Designed with Shermaine Boh and Sen Fong Ling
